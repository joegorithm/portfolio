More information about this thing.

There are over 43 quintillion possible positions of the pieces on a Rubik’s Cube. Only one arrangement is correct. Thankfully, there are many manageable methods that can be used to solve this puzzle and get all the faces back to their original solid color. Having a unique life-cycle, this product surprised many people. When the brain behind the Cube made the invention in the twentieth century, there was evidently a market for this spatial logic toy, which many adaptions and new versions stemmed from, contributing to the overall success of the Rubik’s Cube.
